# ADR-2026-007: Revenue Sharing Deferred as Separate Product Decision

**Date:** 2026-06-21
**Status:** ACCEPTED
**Deciders:** Architecture Council
**Tags:** revenue-sharing, stripe-connect, marketplace, deferred, product-strategy

---

## Context

The gap analysis ranked Revenue Sharing #12 in the Priority Implementation Order, noting it
"requires Stripe Connect; separate product decision." This ADR records the architecture council's
formal position.

Revenue sharing in an LMS context means: when a church (tenant) monetizes a course to its members,
a portion of the revenue flows to ChurchCore LMS as a platform fee, and the remainder goes to the
church's Stripe account.

Implementing this requires:
1. **Stripe Connect** — each church org becomes a Stripe Connect account (either Standard or Express).
   This requires per-country compliance review, identity verification, and ongoing payout management.
2. **Marketplace legal entity** — ChurchCore must operate as a marketplace intermediary, with the
   associated legal, tax (1099-K in the US), and banking obligations.
3. **Pricing model definition** — what % does ChurchCore take? Is it flat-rate or tiered? Is it
   only on paid courses? What about free courses from paid orgs?
4. **Trust & safety layer** — dispute resolution, refund policies, chargebacks split between
   platform and connected account.
5. **AGPL license review** — the AGPL license applies to the open-source code. If ChurchCore
   operates as a SaaS marketplace, the license implications for tenants who modify the code need
   review.

None of these are engineering decisions — they are product strategy, legal, and business model decisions.

---

## Decision

Revenue sharing is **not a feature to be implemented** under the current roadmap. It is a product
strategy decision that requires a separate business and legal process before any code is written.

The council will not generate a COUNCIL implementation document for Revenue Sharing until:
1. A product strategy document defines the business model (fee %, Stripe Connect tier, refund policy), AND
2. Legal review of AGPL implications is complete, AND
3. A dedicated product owner signs off on the pricing model

The existing `organizations.stripe_customer_id` column (added in 20260621100000) supports the
current direct-billing model (church pays ChurchCore directly via Stripe Billing) and must NOT
be repurposed for Stripe Connect without a migration and a new council session.

---

## Consequences

**Enables:**
- Focus on learner-facing and teacher-facing features in Sprint 2–3

**Constrains:**
- No Stripe Connect implementation until the business model is formally decided
- `organizations.stripe_customer_id` is reserved for platform billing, not peer-to-peer payouts
- Any revenue-related feature (e.g., paid course enrollment) that doesn't require peer payouts
  can be built separately using existing Stripe Checkout infrastructure without triggering this ADR

**Deferred rationale on record:** Revenue sharing is a product and legal decision, not an engineering decision.
Engineering can implement it once the product strategy is defined.
